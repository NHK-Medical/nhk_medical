What is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder characterized by repeated pauses in breathing during sleep. These pauses, called apneas, occur when the muscles in the back of the throat fail to keep the airway open, leading the brain to briefly awaken to restore normal breathing

Symptoms:

Loud snoring

choking during sleep

Excessive daytime sleepiness

Morning headaches

Difficulty concentrating

Health Risks:

High blood pressure

Cardiovascular issues

Diabetes

Mood disturbances

Daytime accidents

How to manage Sleep Apnea?

Lifestyle Changes: Weight loss, avoiding alcohol/sedatives, regular exercise, sleeping on the side.

Oral Appliances: Custom-fitted devices reposition the jaw and tongue.Surgery: Considered for severe cases, removes excess tissue or supports the airway.

PAP Devices: Positive Airway Pressure (PAP) devices have revolutionized the treatment of sleep disorders, particularly obstructive sleep apnea.

You might need a sleep study if you experience symptoms like excessive daytime sleepiness, loud snoring, restless legs, pauses in breathing during sleep, or difficulty falling asleep. It helps diagnose sleep disorders like sleep apnea, insomnia, narcolepsy, and restless legs syndrome. 

During an in-lab sleep study, you'll spend a night at a sleep center where electrodes and sensors are attached to your body to monitor different aspects of your sleep. Alternatively, home sleep tests involve using portable devices to monitor your sleep in your own bed.

Sleep apnea is a common sleep disorder where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. It can lead to poor sleep quality and serious health issues if left untreated.

A PAP (Positive Airway Pressure) device is a medical device used to treat sleep apnea. It delivers a constant stream of air pressure to keep the airway open during sleep, preventing breathing interruptions.

There are mainly two types: CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) and BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure). CPAP delivers a constant air pressure, while BiPAP adjusts between higher and lower pressures based on your breathing patterns. 

A PAP device delivers a gentle stream of air through a mask worn over your nose, mouth, or both. This air pressure acts like a splint to keep the airway open, preventing it from collapsing during sleep.

The type of PAP device you need depends on your sleep study results and your doctor's recommendation. Factors like your sleep disorder, comfort preferences, and any other medical conditions will be considered.

It can take some time to get used to a PAP device. Many people experience an adjustment period where they need to find the right mask fit, adjust to the sensation of air pressure, and establish a consistent sleep routine

Using a PAP device as prescribed can significantly improve your sleep quality and alleviate symptoms of sleep apnea. It reduces the risk of associated health problems like heart disease, stroke, and diabetes. 

 Regular cleaning of the mask, tubing, and humidifier chamber (if applicable) is essential to prevent bacteria buildup. Follow the manufacturer's instructions and your healthcare provider's recommendations for cleaning and maintenance.

The duration of PAP therapy depends on your sleep disorder and its severity. Some individuals may need to use it long-term, while others might find their condition improves over time with other treatments.
